# Vision

Anon Wall is a city-based anonymous digital wall for short text posts.

The first target user is people roughly 18-35 who want a lightweight way to read what people nearby are thinking and post a short thought of their own without creating an account.

The MVP solves a simple problem: there is no fast, low-friction place to casually read and share short local thoughts tied to the user's city.

## Why people return

Users come back because they want to know:

- what people are feeling today
- city mood
- weird notes
- hidden gems
- collective consciousness

## Psychological positioning

The product serves a need most apps do not:

- less performance
- less identity
- less algorithms
- more humanity
- small thoughts
- local presence

This is what many people secretly want from social software.

## Tagline candidates

- "The mood of your city, written by strangers."
- "A living wall of local thoughts."
- "Anonymous notes from people around you."

## Version 1 scope

Version 1 is intentionally narrow:

- one public wall per detected city
- no login
- no manual city switching
- text-only posts up to 120 characters
- random pseudonym per browser session
- posts expire after 48 hours

## Localization

The product will be used in Spanish-speaking markets including Mexico. Any feature that surfaces user-facing copy — reactions, prompts, city pulse messages — must support Spanish from the start. English and Spanish are the two launch languages.
